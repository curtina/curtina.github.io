I am a 2nd year PhD Candidate at the Trottier Space Insitute at McGill University working under Victoria Kaspi. My work largely focuses on the study of fast radio bursts, pulsars, and gamma-ray bursts. In addition to research, I am an avid science communicator.

TLDR Research Interests: Fast radio bursts, Pulsars, Radio Telescopes, Magnetars, Gamma-ray bursts, Galactic magnetic field, Very Long Baseline Interferometry

Research

I am a memmber of the CHIME/FRB collaboration. My research within the group has three primary directions: 1. Looking for high-energy counterparts to FRBs, 2. Understanding morphological differences between repeating and non-repeating FRBs, and 3. Callibrating the upcoming CHIME/Outriggers.

In a bit more detail, I have led multiple campaigns (including one published paper and one in prep.) to search for gamma-ray bursts (GRBs) which might be coincident with FRBs. I have also developed an algorithm to use CHIME/FRB to constrain radio emission from transients such as GRBs. In terms of my second interest, I have recently start a project using high-time resolution data collected by the CHIME/FRB telescope to study the morphology of all-know repeating FRBs. I am also helping to build and comission the CHIME/FRB Outriggers, with my primary role being to localize ~100 pulsars to ~mas precision such that we can use them as calibrators for the Outriggers. To localize these pulsars, I have obtained ~250 hours with the VLBA to not only determine positions, but also proper motion and parallax for this sample. This will be the largest published sample of proper motion and parallax.

Lastly, I am the co-leader of the data quality monitoring team for CHIME/FRB. My masters focused on investigating and characterizing the radio frequency interference at CHIME.

Outside of CHIME, I am working on a project with Professor Joel Weisberg and Joanna Rankin to map the structure of the magnetic field within the Milky Way using rotation measures from pulsars.

Science Communication

I spend a significant amount of time on communicating science to different audiences. I am a writer and the lead of the social media team for AstroBites, a graduate-student led community which writes accessible astronomy bites. Checkout all my pieces written on Astrobites. I am also one of four physics outreach coordinators at McGill. Three years ago, I re-designed a number of modules for elementary schools to be more accessible and hands-on. For the past three years, I have coordinated and run these activities throughout Montreal. I also co-founded (along with two other Staff members at McGill) a program with Dell Technologies in which middle school girls build telescopes in Minecraft (see accompanying image for a run of this program).
