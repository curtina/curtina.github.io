Research

My primary research area is that of fast radio bursts. To study these engimatic sources, I am a member of the CHIME/FRB collaboration. More specifically, I am interested in understanding whether fast radio bursts have any high-energy counterparts. As such, I have correlated fast radio burst and gamma-ray burst catalogs and then set limits on FRB-like radio emission from gamma-ray bursts. I am also working on a new project to understand the differences between repeating and apparently non-repeating FRBs using high-time resolution data from the CHIME/FRB telescope. As a part of the CHIME/FRB collaboration, I am also a co-leader of our data quality monitoring team, and have done significant investigations (my MsC) into RFI at CHIME/FRB.

In addition to the above two projects, I am also helping to build and comission the CHIME/FRB Outriggers, with my primary role being to localize ~100 pulsars to ~mas precision such that we can use them as calibrators for the Outriggers. To localize these pulsars, I am using the VLBA. In addition to positions, I will also determine proper motion and parallax for this sample.

Lastly, I am working on a project from undergrad with Professor Joel Weisberg to map the structure of the magnetic field within the Milky Way using rotation measures from pulsars.

TLDR Research Interests: Fast radio bursts, Pulsars, Radio Telescopes, Magnetars, Gamma-ray bursts, Galactic magnetic field, Very Long Baseline Interferometry
