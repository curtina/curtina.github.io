McGill Physics Outreach

Space Explorers

Space Explorers is a program that brings hands-on physics activity to elementary schools throughout Montreal. While Space Explorers was founded in 2017, it underwent a major revamping durign the pandemic. I along with another McGill Graduate student developed new modules that could be performed at home, and were highly inquiry-learning based. We created three new modules and re-structured one existing module. The four modules cover: density, gravity, air resistance and momentum through topics such as boats, parachutes, and craters on the moon.

I was ran the online version of the Space Explorers program for ~1 year, and then the in-person program for two years. While I passed off the program in 2023, I continue to volunteer and provide guidance on the program.

Science in Space: How to Telescope

Science in Space: How to Telescope is a program in which middle school girls build telescopes in Minecraft over the course of ~10 weeks. The program aims to foster a sense of belonging in physics for students of marganlized genders in this domain. Over the course of the 10 weeks, students learn basic astronomy and telescope concepts, and also hone and develop their problem-solving and team-working skills.

This program was founded in 2021 as a collaboration between McGill Physics, Trottier Space Institute, and Dell Technologies/Girls Who Game. It was founded by myself, two McGill Staff members, and a contact at Dell Technologies. This program is unique in its partnership between industry and academia. We piloted the program in Spring 2022 and Spring 2023. We will run at five different elementary schools throughout Montreal in Fall 2023/Spring 2024.

You can find out more about Science in Space at our website.

Astrobites

Astrobites is an international and entirely graduate student-run astronomy news platform. Most articles summarize in bite-sized pieces cutting-edge astronomy papers. Summaries are aimed at undergraduate students, and Astrobites is well known in many astronomy undergraduate classrooms. More information on Astrobites, along with its articles, can be found here.

Writer

I became a writer for Astrobites in Febuary 2021, and was the first writer from a Canadian institution. In order for new writers to join astrobites, each Astrobites rotation only lasts for two years. Over this period, I wrote pieces on fast radio bursts, pulsars, searches for extraterrestrial intelligence, and many other topics. My articles can be found here.

Committee Memberships

While I am now longer in the two year Astrobites writing rotation, I serve on multiple committees for Astrobites. Each year, Astrobites receives ~60-100 applications for ~20 spots. For the last three years, I have served on the Astrobites Hiring Committee. I have also served on the Astrobites Climate Committee, which focuses on the role astronomers play in climate change, for the last three years. Recently, I have joined the admin committee.

In addition to serving on committees, I was a co-chair of the social media committee for two years, and am now the sole chair. In this committee, we aim to share our Astrobites summaries, and astronomy news in general, through different media forms. Something new and exciting in this realm are Astrobite reels which you can find on our Instagram.

Equity, Diversity, and Inclusion Work

I am committed to creating equitable and inclusive environments in physics and astronomy. This commitment drives a lot of the science communication and outreach that I do. For example, there is significant research that shows that attrition in physics can start as young as elementary school. Science in Space was founded, in part, to help mitigate this attrition for students of minority genders in physics.

In addition to the outreach I discussed above, I also served on two committees related to Equity, Diveristy, and Inclusion within the McGill Physics Department from Spring of 2020 to Spring 2021. The goal of the two committees were to create a statement on Equity, Diversity, and Inclusion for the department, and to develop an action plan related to this statement. The final values statement can be found here. I also helped in developing a resource page for McGill students, which can be found here.
